About Me

I am currently pursuing doctoral degree from Indian Institute of Technology Delhi, India. I am working under the supervision of Prof. Tarak Karmakar. My PhD thesis research is focused on understanding the self-assembly of nanoclusters, their interactions with biomolecules and mechanistic investigations of their formation and their catalytic activities. I use molecular dynamics simulations to study the time evolution of molecular systems, which allows me to gain insights into their structural and dynamical behaviors of nanoclusters. In addition, I employ enhanced sampling techniques such as metadynamics and OPES to explore rare events and accurately compute underlying free energy landscapes. Further, I leverage machine learning methods such as deepMD to model nanocluster in solution with DFT level accuracy at a fraction of cost. This interdisciplinary approach helps me bridge the gap between traditional computational chemistry and modern data-driven techniques, pushing the boundaries of what we understand about nanoscale phenomena and catalysis.
